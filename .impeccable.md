## Design Context

### Users
This portfolio is for public visitors who want to understand the creator through work, ideas, and experience rather than through a conventional hiring funnel. The main audience includes hiring managers, developers, and collaborators, but the primary job of the site is not conversion into a job application. The site should act as a showcase of experience, knowledge, projects, and thinking.

### Brand Personality
Modern, unique, creative. The interface should feel authored and memorable, not like a generic portfolio template or AI-generated landing page. It should communicate range, duality, and originality, similar to the split-personality idea already present in the hero.

### Aesthetic Direction
Support both light and dark themes equally. Keep the hero and navigation concept intact because they already express the creative theme well. Use the provided reference images as layout inspiration, especially for the profile and blogs pages, but do not copy their colors directly. Favor editorial structure, asymmetry, sharper typographic hierarchy, and distinctive compositions over repetitive card grids.

### Design Principles
- Preserve the existing creative duality in the hero and extend that sense of intention to the rest of the site.
- Make every page feel curated and layout-led rather than component-led.
- Unify repeated UI primitives so chips, tags, and buttons feel like one system.
- Use typography, spacing, and contrast to create identity before adding decorative effects.
- Avoid generic portfolio patterns, safe SaaS styling, and visual decisions that read as AI-generated.
